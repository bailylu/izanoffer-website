Master of Science Programmes

The Master of Science (Laurea Magistrale) is a second-cycle degree programme lasting 2 years and equivalent to 120 ECTS credits. The University offers a comprehensive catalogue of postgraduate programmes across three Schools, designed to prepare graduates for academic, industrial and creative careers at international level.

Three Schools

The School of Engineering offers 20 Master of Science programmes covering disciplines such as mechanical, electrical, energy, civil, computer and management engineering. Sixteen of these programmes are delivered entirely in English.

The School of Architecture, Urban Planning, Construction Engineering offers 11 Master of Science programmes, including programmes in Architectural Design, Building Engineering and Urban Planning.

The School of Design offers 18 Master of Science programmes encompassing product design, communication design, fashion design, interior design and integrated product design.

Information for International Applicants

Across the three Schools, a total of 49 Master of Science programmes are offered, of which 33 are taught entirely in English and open to applicants from outside the European Union. The standard application window for non-EU candidates runs annually from November to March.

Candidates are expected to hold a Bachelor's degree (or equivalent) of at least three years of full-time study, achieved with strong academic standing. Specific language requirements and prerequisite coursework vary by programme.
